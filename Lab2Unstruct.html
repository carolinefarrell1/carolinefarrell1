##Problem 3 Sir Arthur Conan Doyle is well known for his book “The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes”. A little-known fact about him is that he was also a physician who loved poetry. Three of his published poetic works are part of the project Gutenberg archives. Find and list them (sorted by title).

gutenberg_works() %>% 
  filter(author == "Doyle, Arthur Conan") %>% 
  select(gutenberg_id, title) %>% 
  left_join(gutenberg_subjects, by = "gutenberg_id") %>% 
  filter(str_detect(str_to_lower(subject), "poetry")) %>% 
  select(title) %>% 
  arrange(title)

##Problem 9 With the use of a stacked bar chart, show the ratio of negative to positive words in each chapter of the book. Use the Bing lexicon for this.

sense_book %>% 
  inner_join(get_sentiments("bing"), by = "word") %>% 
  count(chapter, sentiment) %>% 
  ggplot(mapping = aes(x = chapter, y = n, fill = sentiment)) + 
  geom_col() + 
  labs(x = "Chapter", y = "Sentiment Score") + theme_minimal()

##Problem 10 Create a series of smoothed line plots within a single chart that show trendlines of how often “trust”, “anticipation”, “joy”, “disgust”, “surprise” and “anger” sentiment words are used in each chapter of the book.

sense_book %>% 
  inner_join(get_sentiments("nrc"), by = "word") %>% 
  filter(sentiment %in% c("anger", "anticipation", "joy", "disgust", "surprise", "trust")) %>% 
  count(chapter, sentiment) %>% 
  ggplot(mapping = aes(x = chapter, y = n, color = sentiment)) + 
  geom_smooth(se = FALSE) + 
  theme_minimal()
